How confident are you one these problem solving methods?

Pseudocode

I'm not sure I completely understand Pseudocode yet! However the practise of writing out the steps you want code to follow and breaking a problem down step by step is very helpful!

Trying something

Writing and testing code in online platforms where I can console.log has been super helpful! I have had the chance to play around with and try different approaches. This gave me insight into how each step of the code works and what is static and what can be changed.

Rubber Ducky Method

I feel like I am the rubber duck most of the time... however pairing up and learning from/teaching other people who have similar understanding levels as me was super helpful. Explaining it to myself, other people or a 'rubber duck', really helps the information to sink in.

Reading Error Messages

I honestly haven't spent enough time reading error messages in the terminal. This is something I want to do more. I can get into the habbit of just paying around till it works. Reading the error messages in VSCode however are very helpful. The red squiggly line has corrected me multiple times.

Console.logging

LOVE this. Console.logging is my absolute favourite way to test if what i'm doing is right. It is the difference between knowing what you're creating and just fumbling round in the dark.

Googling

Google is epic! It's becoming a coding best friend!

Asking for help from peers

Ever better best friend! I can't say enough how much I appreciate the help my peers have given so far!

Asking for help from coaches

I haven't done this so much yet! But will keep the option in mind! Going to the live sessions and asking questions in there has been very useful!

Improving proccess through reflection

This comes through when doing peer programming. Gives the chance to reflect with someone on how we're going, whats hard and strengths!

Reflection on a time you were reluctant to ask for help?

I often find once I've had help I set myself a made up window of not being allowed to ask for help again. I think worrying that it will mean I can't do 'anything' by myself. However the more I ask for help, the more I learn and the more likely I am to be able to do things by myself.
